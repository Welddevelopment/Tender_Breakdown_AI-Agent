## The story (current draft — 2026-07-04)

### How did your group meet?

The three of them found each other before they found me.

Joel and Jawad have been shipping together for over a year — they built weld (a
marketplace matching Roblox developers to studios) as Joel's *first ever* coding
project, then took 2nd place together at Lightfern's GTM Hack. Joel and Pranav
co-run an 11+ tutoring agency with paying customers. By the time I met them, they
weren't hackathon acquaintances — they were a functioning team with a shipping
history, a revenue-generating business, and hackathon placements, who happened to
be too young to incorporate.

I'm Bobby, 24. I was a trader. Trading is professionally reading dense documents
under deadline where a missed clause costs real money — which is exactly what a
bid manager does with a 200-page UK government tender, except the bid manager does
it by hand and losing means the company doesn't eat. We met at a UK AI hackathon,
built Bidframe in 7 days, and I've been full-time on it since.

### Why this team wins

Every function is covered by someone who has already done it for real, not in theory:

- **Joel (15) runs growth at a YC S26 company.** He got that job by cold-emailing
  unsolicited growth audits to founders until one hired him. He placed 2nd in two
  sponsor categories at Multiagents Hack London as the youngest person in the
  building. LinkedIn banned his account at 1,000 followers — for being underage.
  He owns GTM.
- **Jawad (15) has done paid design and 3D work since he was 13.** He runs his own
  studio and is CMO of a games-M&A company. He owns brand and product design.
- **Pranav (15) runs a tutoring business** — the only founder who has actually
  operated the kind of SME that's locked out of public procurement today. He owns
  sales ops.
- **Bobby (24) owns the engine** — extraction, reconciliation, evidence-grounded
  autofill — and is the full-time founder of record and CEO.

We are not a team that formed to apply to YC. We're a team that kept ending up on
the same winners' podiums until incorporating was the obvious next step.
