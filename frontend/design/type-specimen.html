Pick the Bidframe typeface

Every card below is the same little slice of Bidframe, set in a different pair of fonts. Nothing changes except the type, so you can trust your eye. Scroll, see which screen you would be happy to demo, and tell me the number.

Three jobs, three kinds of font: a heading face (the warm bit you read first), a body face (the workhorse, used everywhere), and a fixed-width evidence face for source text like Section 4.2.1 so it always reads as the document talking, not us.

my recommendation, if you want it decided

Telma for headings, Chillax for body

It is card 1. Both are the faces you already liked, both are free on Fontshare, and together they are warm and distinctive without looking like every other AI app. It honours Paper and Forest.

One honest caveat: Chillax is rounded, so in the densest matrix rows it is a touch softer than a plain workhorse sans. If that ever bothers us mid build, card 2 keeps Telma headings and swaps the body to Hanken Grotesk, which is built for small dense text. That is a one line change, so we are not locked in.

Switching later is one change: the whole system reads from two variables, --font-head and --font-body, plus --font-mono for evidence. Fonts load from Fontshare (Chillax, Telma) and Google Fonts (the rest); all free for commercial use. Evidence stays IBM Plex Mono whichever pairing wins.
