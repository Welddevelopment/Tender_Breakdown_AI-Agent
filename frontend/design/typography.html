Fraunces and Chillax

Calm warmth, not loud warmth. Fraunces gives the headings a soft, considered, paper-document feel; Chillax keeps everything underneath friendly and human. The pairing is distinctive without looking like every other tool, and it sits inside Paper and Forest.

Numbers — the one place Chillax steps aside

Confidence is never a number, so most digits in Bidframe are page references, clause numbers and IDs. Those are source facts, so we set them in the mono evidence style. That keeps columns aligned, sidesteps any worry about rounded figures in a dense table, and reinforces the "this is the document talking" idea.

Holding the line (ties into the slop check)

DoKeep headings short. Fraunces earns its warmth in a few words, not a long sentence.
Don'tNo really long headlines. No thin Chillax weights on paper, they go muddy.
DoUse the serif sparingly. Headings and the odd hero number only, never in dense UI.
Don'tNo gradient text, no all-caps sentences, no third or fourth size sneaking in.
DoNumbers and source refs go in the mono evidence style so columns line up.
Don'tNo raw confidence figures like 0.92. Confidence is the dot, never a digit.
The whole system reads from three variables: --font-head (Fraunces), --font-body (Chillax), --font-mono (IBM Plex Mono). Two weights of Fraunces, three of Chillax, kept deliberately small. If Chillax ever feels soft in the very densest rows during the build, swapping --font-body to Hanken Grotesk is a one line change and nothing else moves.
